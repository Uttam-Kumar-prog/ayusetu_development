The platform integrates four core modules: a real-time telemedicine consultation system powered by WebRTC, a structured Panchakarma therapy scheduling engine, an AI-based NLP symptom checker supporting text and voice input in Englis h, Hindi, and Punjabi, and a real-time pharmacy inventory visibility module. AyuSetu directly addresses these disparities by enabling specialist consultations from any location, digitizing therapy management, and providing pre-consultation AI triage. The platform's hybrid Phygital model serves both the tech-savvy young adult demographic (80% telemedicine preference) and the middle-aged demographic that drives demand for traditional Ayurvedic care (70% preference). modern medical access in rural India and traditional Ayurvedic therapy management. The core goals are: Democratize Healthcare Access: To provide rural pa tients with immediate access to qualified specialists via video/audio consultations, and to reduce the financial burden and time lost in travelling long distances for basic medical advice. Modernize Ayurveda: To digitize the currently unstructured manageme nt of Panchakarma therapies and to move from manual paper records to automated scheduling, tracking, and patient notifications. Unified Care Model: To bring Allopathic (Modern) and Ayurvedic (Traditional) treatments under a single Phygital (Physical + Digi tal) roof, allowing patients to switch seamlessly between consultation types based on their needs. Empower Stakeholders: To enable doctors to manage remote practices efficiently and to help local pharmacies increase visibility by displaying real-time medic ine stock to nearby patients. 1.2 DESCRIPTION OF THE PROJECT AyuSetu is a web-based platform designed to address specific challenges in India's healthcare sector. It is not merely a booking application; it is a complete healthcare management system serving multiple stakeholder types simultaneously. For Patients: It acts as a digital health companion. A patient in a village can consult a city doctor, book a 7-day Ayurvedic detox therapy, and check if their prescribed medicine is available at the nearest pharmacy — all from one phone. For Practitioners: It serves as a practice management tool. Ayurvedic Vaidyas can manage complex, multi-day therapy schedules without overlapping appointments. For Infrastructure: It includes an offline-first architecture. Recognizing that rural internet is unstable, the system allows key data (such as health records and therapy schedules) to be accessed without an active connection. AI Assistance: An integrated AI symptom checker helps patients triage their own condition using simple voice or text inputs in vernacular languages. 1.3 SCOPE OF THE PROJECT In-Scope: Patient Module: Registration, Search, Booking, and Health History. CHAPTER 2: PROFILE OF THE PROBLEM — RATIONALE &amp; SCOPE 2.1 PROBLEM STATEMENT India's healthcare system is characterized by a profound structural imbalance between rural and ur ban regions. Despite advances in digital infrastructure, approximately 65% of India's population residing in rural areas continues to face severe barriers to accessing timely, affordable, and quality healthcare. The primary challenges include a critical sh ortage of qualified medical professionals in underserved regions, the complete absence of digitized management systems for traditional Ayurvedic therapies, and the lack of integration between modern allopathic medicine and traditional healing practices wit hin a single accessible platform. The Panchakarma therapy management domain remains entirely dependent on manual, paper-based scheduling systems. Ayurvedic practitioners maintain patient therapy schedules in physical diaries, which are susceptible to sche duling conflicts, missed sessions, and the absence of follow-up mechanisms, resulting in suboptimal treatment adherence. Concurrently, rural patients seeking specialist consultations must travel distances of 20 to 50 kilometres, incurring significant finan cial burden and time loss. The absence of any pre-consultation triage mechanism further compounds inefficiency in specialist utilization. 2.2 RATIONALE FOR THE PROJECT The rationale for developing AyuSetu rests on three foundational pillars: Digital Bridg ing of Healthcare Disparity: Telemedicine has been demonstrated globally to reduce specialist consultation costs by up to 40% and eliminate travel barriers. AyuSetu applies this proven model with rural India-specific adaptations, including offline function ality and vernacular language support. Modernization of Traditional Medicine: India's AYUSH sector serves millions of patients annually, yet operates without standardized digital management tools. AyuSetu addresses this gap by providing structured digital workflows for Panchakarma therapy scheduling, progress tracking, and automated reminders. Integrated Phygital Care Model: The convergence of physical Ayurvedic therapies and digital consultation platforms under a unified ecosystem represents an unexplored opportunity. AyuSetu's hybrid model ensures that patients can transition seamlessly between telemedicine and traditional therapy. The project also aligns strategically with the Government of India's Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) and National Digi tal Health Mission (NDHM), which seek to establish a comprehensive digital health ecosystem. AyuSetu's architecture is designed to support eventual integration with the ABDM framework. 2.3 SCOPE OF THE STUDY The scope of the AyuSetu study encompasses the following domains: Healthcare Technology: Application of web technologies, WebRTC, and PWA architecture to healthcare delivery. Natural Language Processing: Application of NLP models for multilingual, low-bandwidth medical symptom analysis. Traditional Medicine Digitization: Digital workflow design for multi-day Ayurvedic Panchakarma therapy management. Rural Technology Adoption: U ser experience design principles for low-literacy, low-bandwidth user populations. Health Informatics: Design of data models for integrated patient health records across multiple treatment modalities. CHAPTER 3: EXISTING SYSTEM 3.1 INTRODUCTION TO THE EXISTING SYSTEM The existing healthcare delivery system in rural and semi-urban India i s characterized by fragmentation, manual processes, and a near-complete absence of digital integration. Healthcare access relies predominantly on physical visits to primary health centres (PHCs), private clinics, and government hospitals, which are geograp hically concentrated in urban areas. This section examines the existing system across key operational dimensions and identifies its critical limitations. 3.2 EXISTING PROCESSES AND LIMITATIONS 3.2.1 Patient Consultation Patients initiate healthcare by phy sically travelling to the nearest facility. The absence of digital appointment scheduling results in queuing times of 2–6 hours. Doctors lack access to prior digital health history, necessitating repetitive anamnesis at each visit. No post-consultation fol low-up or reminder mechanism exists. 3.2.2 Panchakarma Therapy Management Ayurvedic Panchakarma therapy management is conducted entirely via manual, paper-based registers. This results in scheduling conflicts, missed therapy sessions with no reminder mech anism, absence of systematic progress tracking, and inaccessible historical records without physical access to registers. 3.2.3 Pharmacy and Medicine Access Patients must physically visit multiple pharmacies to ascertain stock availability. No centralized system communicates real-time inventory status to patients or doctors, resulting in unnecessary patient movement and delayed treatment initiation. 3.3 EXISTING SOFTWARE SYSTEMS Several digital health platforms operate in the Indian market. The process initiates when an authenticated patient submits a booking request. Input validation occurs first, followed by real-time slot availability verification against the doctor_availability table. A concurrency-safe slot locking mechanism (database-level pessimistic locking via Redis) prevents simultaneous booki ngs. Upon successful lock, appointment details are persisted, a meeting link is generated, and asynchronous notification tasks are dispatched. On slot conflict, the system releases the lock and returns an availability refresh, redirecting the patient to se lect an alternative slot. START: Patient submits booking request ↓ DECISION: Is patient session valid? YES ↓ NO → Return 401 Unauthorized DECISION: Is slot available? (DB Query) YES ↓ NO → Release lock; Notify patient; Redirect to slot selection PROCESS: Acquire pessimistic lock on slot (Redis) ↓ PROCESS: Save appointment + Generate meeting link ↓ PROCESS: Dispatch async notifications (SMS + Push) ↓ END: Return booking confirmation to patient Figure 6.5: Flowchart – Appointment Booking Process 6.6 PSEUDO CODE – APPOINTMENT BOOKING MODULE FUNCTION bookAppointment(patientId, doctorId, slotId): // Step 1: Validate session IF NOT isValidSession(patientId) THEN RETURN Error('401: Unauthorized - Invalid session') END IF IF NOT slotExists(slotId) THEN RETURN Error('404: Slot not found') END IF // Step 2: Begin database transaction BEGIN TRANSACTION // Step 3: Acquire pessimistic lock on slot (SELECT FOR UPDATE) slot = lockSlot(slotId) IF slot.status != 'AVAILABLE' THEN ROLLBACK TRANSACTION RETURN Error('409: Slot no longer available - please select another') END IF // Step 4: Update slot status to BOOKED UPDATE slot SET status = 'BOOKED', lockedBy = patientId // Step 5: Create appointment record appointmentId = generateUUID() meetingLink = generateMeetingLink(appointmentId) INSERT INTO appointments ( appointmentId, patientId, doctorId, slotId, meetingLink, status ) VALUES (appointmentId, patientId, doc torId, slotId, meetingLink, 'CONFIRMED') // Step 6: Commit transaction COMMIT TRANSACTION // Step 7: Dispatch async notifications (non-blocking) QUEUE notifyPatient(patientId, appointmentId) QUEUE notifyDoctor(doctorId, appointmentId) // Step 8: Return success response RETURN Success({ appointmentId : appointmentId, meetingLink : meetingLink, status : 'CONFIRMED' }) EXCEPTION DatabaseError: ROLLBACK TRANSACTION LOG error details with timestamp RETURN Error('500: Booking failed - please try again') END FUNCTION Figure 6.6: Pseudocode – Appointment Booking with Concurrency Control 6.7 USER INTERFACE DESIGN A key focus of the project is providing a clean, accessible, and modern interface. Each sprint delivers a demonstrable increment of functional software, validated against the sprint backlog. The development team operat es using a feature-branch Git workflow with mandatory peer code review before merge to the main development branch. The backend follows a RESTful microservices-influenced architecture, with the AI symptom checker deployed as a separate Python Flask servic e. This separation ensures that computationally intensive NLP inference does not impact the performance of core booking and consultation APIs. The frontend is developed as a Progressive Web App, enabling a single codebase to deliver both a web application and a mobile app-like experience without requiring App Store or Play Store distribution — a critical accessibility advantage for rural users. Database interactions use parameterized queries throughout, preventing SQL injection. All API endpoints are prote cted by JWT middleware with role-based access control (RBAC) enforcing module-level authorization (patient/doctor/pharmacy/admin). 8.3 CONVERSION PLAN The transition from the existing manual system to AyuSetu follows a staged migration strategy: Stage 1 – Parallel Operation (Weeks 1–4 post-launch): The digital platform operates alongside existing manual processes. Practitioners continue maintaining physical registers while simultaneously logging sessions in AyuSetu, enabling cross-validation and confidence building. Stage 2 – Digital Primary, Manual Backup (Weeks 5–8): AyuSetu becomes the primary record and scheduling system. Manual records serve as backup. Structured training sessions are conducted for all staff. Stage 3 – Full Digital Transition (Week 9 onwards): Manual records are discontinued. Historical paper records are digitized through retrospective data entry supported by a bulk import tool. The system enters full production operation. 8.4 POST-IMPLEMENTAT ION AND SOFTWARE MAINTENANCE Corrective Maintenance: Bug reports submitted via in-app feedback are triaged weekly. The requirement for voice navigation and the Panchakarma scheduling gap were bo th discovered through direct stakeholder engagement. Offline-First Must Be Architected from Day Zero: Retrofitting offline capability onto an existing online-first architecture is prohibitively complex. The decision to adopt a PWA offline-first approach fr om the initial architecture phase was critical and saved significant rework. NLP for Low-Resource Languages Requires Custom Training Data: Pre-trained English NLP models exhibit significantly degraded performance on Hinglish (Hindi-English code-mixed) medi cal symptom descriptions, necessitating a domain-specific multilingual training dataset. Database Concurrency Must Be Explicitly Designed: The appointment booking double-booking issue, discovered during integration testing, demonstrated that concurrent acc ess scenarios must be explicitly designed at the data model level, not addressed as an afterthought. Scope Management Is Critical: Several initially proposed features (in-app payment, medicine delivery logistics) were correctly identified and excluded from Phase 1 scope, preserving development focus and timeline adherence. CHAPTER 10: USER MANUAL 10.1 INTRODUCTION This User Manual provides step-by-step instructions for using the AyuSetu platform. It is intended for three primary user types: Patients, Doctor s, and Pharmacy Operators. The platform is accessible via any web browser on a smartphone or computer — no app installation is required. The application may be added to the device home screen for app-like access by selecting 'Add to Home Screen' from the b rowser menu. 10.2 PATIENT GUIDE 10.2.1 Registration and Login Open your web browser and navigate to the AyuSetu platform URL. On the home screen, tap 'Register' to create a new account. Enter your 10-digit mobile number. Tap 'Send OTP'. Enter the received 6-digit OTP and tap 'Verify'. Complete your profile: name, age, gender, blood group, and language preference. Tap 'Save Profile'. You will be directed to your Patient Dashboard. 10.2.2 Booking a Consultation From the Patient Dashboard, tap 'Find a Doctor '. Apply filters: Specialty (e.g., Ayurveda, General Physician), Location, and Language. Browse matching doctors. Tap a doctor's name to view their profile and ratings. Tap 'Book Appointment' on the doctor's profile. Select an available date and time slot (green slots indicate availability). Review the booking summary and tap 'Confirm Booking'. A confirmation screen will appear and an SMS will be sent with appointment det ails and meeting link. 10.2.3 Using the AI Symptom Checker From the Patient Dashboard, tap 'Check Symptoms'. Type a description of your symptoms in English or Hindi (e.g., 'Sar mein dard aur bukhar 3 din se hai'). Alternatively, tap the microphone icon fo r voice input. Tap 'Analyze Symptoms'. Review the results: predicted condition, urgency level, and recommended next steps. Note: The Symptom Checker is an informational tool only and does not replace professional medical advice. Always consult a qualified doctor for diagnosis and treatment. 10.2.4 Joining a Video Consultation At the scheduled time, open the AyuSetu app and go to 'My Appointments'. Find your appointment and tap 'Join Consultation'. Allow the browser to access your camera and microphone when prompted. You will be connected to your doctor's consultation room. After the consultation, your e-prescription will appear automatically in 'My Prescriptions'. 10.3 DOCTOR GUIDE 10.3.1 Login and Dashboard Navigate to the AyuSetu platform URL and tap 'Doctor Login'. Enter your registered mobile number and authenticate via OTP. The Doctor Dashboard displays today's schedule, pending cases, and practice analytics. 10.3.2 Conducting a Video Consultation At the scheduled consultation time, tap the appointment to open the Patient Case Summary. Review the patient's profile, symptom h istory, and AI-generated case notes. Tap 'Start Consultation' to initiate the video call. Use the side panel to type consultation notes during the call. Tap 'End Consultation' upon completion. In the e-Prescription form, add diagnosed conditions, medicines , dosage, and instructions. Tap 'Generate Prescription PDF'. The prescription is automatically shared with the patient. 10.3.3 Managing Panchakarma Sessions (Ayurvedic Vaidyas) From the Doctor Dashboard, tap 'Therapy Manager'. Today's scheduled therapy se ssions are listed with patient names and therapy types. Tap a session to view patient history and prior session notes. After conducting the session, tap 'Mark as Completed' and optionally add notes. The patient's therapy progress bar updates automatically. 10.4 PHARMACY OPERATOR GUIDE 10.4.1 Updating Medicine Inventory Log in to the Pharmacy Portal using your registered mobile number and OTP. The Inventory Dashboard displays a list of medicines with toggle switches. Tap the toggle next to a medicine name t o switch status: 'Available' (green) or 'Unavailable' (grey). Changes are saved automatically and reflected immediately on the patient-facing view. 10.4.2 Viewing Patient Prescriptions Tap 'Scan Prescription' from the Pharmacy Portal home screen. Use your device camera to scan the QR code on the patient's digital prescription. The prescription details will be displayed: medicines, dosage, and prescribing doctor.
